Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our financial tools and calculators.

Which credit card interest calculator is best for India?

Our APR Shock Tool supports INR with Indian bank APR rates (30–44%). It shows total interest, payoff timeline, and amortization — giving you the full picture of what your balance actually costs.

How do I use the Debt Payoff Planner?

Enter each debt's name, balance, APR, and minimum payment. Set an extra monthly budget. The tool compares Snowball (smallest balance first) vs Avalanche (highest rate first) and shows total interest saved for each strategy.

Are these tools free and do they store my data?

All 16 tools are completely free. All calculations happen in your browser — no data is sent to any server, no sign-up required, and nothing is stored. Your financial details never leave your device.

What is a good CIBIL score for a home loan in India?

750+ is considered excellent and qualifies you for the best rates. 700–749 is good — most banks approve with slightly higher rates. Our Credit Score Simulator shows exactly which actions will push your score above the 750 threshold.

How much emergency fund should I have in India?

Standard recommendation is 6 months of essential expenses. Our Emergency Fund Planner calculates your exact target, shows current survival time, and creates a month-by-month savings plan to reach full coverage.

What is the 50/30/20 budget rule?

50% of take-home pay goes to needs (rent, food, EMIs), 30% to wants (dining, entertainment), and 20% to savings and investments. Our Budget Optimizer applies this rule visually with a donut chart and smart nudges for overspending.
