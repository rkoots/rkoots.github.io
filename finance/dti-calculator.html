Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good DTI ratio for a home loan in India?

Most Indian banks prefer a DTI ratio below 40–43% for home loans. RBI guidelines suggest total EMI obligations should not exceed 50% of net monthly income. A DTI below 30% gives you the best chances and interest rates.

How is DTI calculated?

DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income) × 100. For example, if your total EMIs are ₹25,000 and your gross income is ₹80,000, your DTI = 31.25%.

Does a high DTI affect credit score?

DTI itself isn't directly used in CIBIL score calculations, but the debts contributing to a high DTI (credit utilization, multiple loans) do affect your score. A high DTI also means lenders view you as a higher credit risk during manual assessment.

How can I lower my DTI ratio quickly?

1) Pay off smaller debts first to eliminate EMIs. 2) Avoid taking new loans before applying. 3) Increase income through side income or declarations. 4) Use a balance transfer to consolidate high-payment debts into a single lower EMI.
